Essay Series · Nine Parts

A Grammar
of the Whole

An inquiry into reality as a nested structure — from the question of what holds everything together to the question of what that means for a single life.

What holds reality together? This series develops an answer in nine steps — from the basic structure of being to the consequences for how we live, know, and create.

Each essay stands alone and carries a thread forward from the one before. The essays work at the boundary between metaphysics and cognitive science, drawing on both without belonging entirely to either.

On the form of these essays

Ladder of abstraction
Each essay moves between the concrete and the universal — a specific observation opens, a structural argument follows, a question closes. Neither rung alone is enough.
Perennial, not topical
These essays are written to be read at any time, from anywhere. They do not comment on current events. They try to reach something prior to the news cycle.
Open tensions
Three structural tensions run through the whole series — teleology without foundation, metaphysics or anthropology, time and causality. Each essay lives with them rather than resolving them prematurely.
